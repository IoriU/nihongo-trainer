# Lesson 01 — Greetings & Self-Introduction (あいさつ と じこしょうかい)

> First real Japanese! By the end of this lesson you can greet people and introduce yourself — the single most useful thing when you arrive in Japan.

---

## 🎯 Goals

By the end you will be able to:
- Greet people at any time of day
- Say your name and where you're from
- Understand the core sentence pattern **「Aは Bです」** (A is B)
- Bow and use さん correctly (real-life etiquette)

---

## 🗣️ Vocabulary — Greetings (あいさつ)

Say each one out loud 3 times. The romaji is a crutch — your hiragana already covers most of these.

| Japanese | Romaji | 🇮🇩 Indonesian | When to use |
|----------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| おはよう ございます | *ohayō gozaimasu* | Selamat pagi | Morning (polite) |
| こんにちは | *konnichiwa* | Selamat siang / halo | Daytime |
| こんばんは | *konbanwa* | Selamat malam | Evening |
| ありがとう ございます | *arigatō gozaimasu* | Terima kasih | Thank you (polite) |
| すみません | *sumimasen* | Maaf / permisi | Sorry / excuse me |
| はい / いいえ | *hai / iie* | Ya / tidak | Yes / no |
| はじめまして | *hajimemashite* | Salam kenal | First meeting only |
| よろしく おねがいします | *yoroshiku onegaishimasu* | (≈ "mohon kerja samanya") | End of self-intro |

⚠️ **こんにちは** is written with は at the end (not わ), even though it sounds like *wa*. Same for **こんばんは**. This is a historical spelling — you'll see why in the grammar section.

---

## 🧩 Grammar

### 1. The big one: 「Aは Bです」 = "A is B"

This is the backbone of beginner Japanese. Three parts:

```
わたし    は      がくせい    です。
watashi   wa      gakusei     desu
(I)       [topic] (student)   (am)
🇮🇩 "Saya adalah pelajar."  →  "I am a student."
```

- **わたし** (*watashi*) = I / saya
- **は** = the **topic particle**. ⚠️ It's the kana `は` but pronounced **wa** here. It marks *what the sentence is about* ("as for me…").
- **です** (*desu*) = polite "am / is / are". One word covers all of them — no conjugation for I/you/he like in English! 🎉

So the pattern is literally:
> **[topic] は [info] です。**

### 2. Word order is different from Indonesian/English

Notice **です comes at the very end**. Japanese verbs and です always go last. Get used to "saving the verb for last" — it's the #1 structural habit to build.

### 3. Making it a question: add か

Add **か** (*ka*) to the end + rising tone. No need to flip word order like English.

```
あなたは がくせいですか。
anata wa gakusei desu ka?
🇮🇩 "Apakah kamu pelajar?"  →  "Are you a student?"
```

- **あなた** (*anata*) = you. ⚠️ In real life Japanese people use the person's **name + さん** instead of あなた (see culture note).

### 4. Saying where you're from: の

```
わたしは インドネシア の しゅっしん です。
watashi wa Indonesia no shusshin desu.
🇮🇩 "Saya berasal dari Indonesia."
```
- **しゅっしん** (*shusshin*) = origin / hometown
- **の** (*no*) links two nouns: `Indonesia の shusshin` = "Indonesia's origin" = "from Indonesia"

---

## 💬 Dialogue — first meeting (はじめての であい)

> Read it aloud, both roles. Cover the romaji and try again.

**A:** はじめまして。
*Hajimemashite.*
🇮🇩 Salam kenal.

**A:** わたしは ハサン です。インドネシア の しゅっしん です。
*Watashi wa Hasan desu. Indonesia no shusshin desu.*
🇮🇩 Saya Hasan. Saya dari Indonesia.

**A:** よろしく おねがいします。
*Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.*

**B:** はじめまして。たなか です。よろしく おねがいします。
*Hajimemashite. Tanaka desu. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.*
🇮🇩 Salam kenal. Saya Tanaka. Mohon kerja samanya.

💡 Notice B just said **「たなか です」** — dropping わたしは is totally natural when it's obvious you're talking about yourself.

---

## 🏯 Culture note — for living in Japan

- **Name order is reversed:** Japanese say *family name first* (たなか = Tanaka is the surname). On forms, 名字 (myōji) = surname, 名前 (namae) = given name.
- **さん (-san)** goes after *other people's* names (たなかさん = Mr./Ms. Tanaka), like *Pak/Bu/Mas/Mbak*. ⚠️ **Never** add さん to your own name — it's like calling yourself "Mr."
- **Bow, don't shake hands.** A small bow (~15°) with はじめまして is standard. A handshake can feel too forward.
- **よろしく おねがいします** has no real English/Indonesian equivalent — it's a social glue phrase meaning roughly "please treat me well / I look forward to our relationship." You'll use it constantly: meeting people, starting a job, even ending emails.

8. Write your own 4-line self-introduction (はじめまして → name → where from → よろしく).

## ✅ Answer Key

**Part A**
1. おはよう ございます。 *(ohayō gozaimasu)*
2. わたしは ハサン です。 *(watashi wa Hasan desu)*
3. あなたは がくせいですか。 *(anata wa gakusei desu ka)* — better in real life: 「（name）さんは がくせいですか。」
4. ありがとう ございます。 *(arigatō gozaimasu)*
5. わたしは インドネシア の しゅっしん です。 *(watashi wa Indonesia no shusshin desu)*

**Part B**
6. ❌ さん is not used on your own name, and the word order is broken. ✅ わたしは たなか です。
7. ❌ Don't put さん on yourself. ✅ わたしは アリ です。

**Part C** — Example:
> はじめまして。
> わたしは （あなたの なまえ） です。
> インドネシア の しゅっしん です。
> よろしく おねがいします。
