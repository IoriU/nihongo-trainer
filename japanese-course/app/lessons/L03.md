# Lesson 03 — Numbers & Prices (すうじ・ねだん) 🏯

> Money, counting, and asking "how much?" — you'll use this every single day in Japan, starting at the konbini.

---

## 🎯 Goals

By the end you will be able to:
- Count **0–10**, then build up to hundreds, thousands, and 万 (ten-thousands)
- Read and say **prices in 円 (yen)**
- Ask **「いくらですか」** (how much is it?)
- Buy something with **「これを ください」**

---

|----------|--------|-----|
| いち | *ichi* | 1 |
| に | *ni* | 2 |
| さん | *san* | 3 |
| よん / し | *yon / shi* | 4 |
| ご | *go* | 5 |

⚠️ **4, 7, 9 have two readings.** For counting and prices, よん・なな・きゅう are the safe defaults.

---

## 🧩 Grammar / How numbers build

### 1. 11–99 — just add the pieces

Japanese numbers are wonderfully regular. No new words needed:

```
11 = じゅういち      (10 + 1)
20 = にじゅう        (2 × 10)
35 = さんじゅうご    (3×10 + 5)
99 = きゅうじゅうきゅう
```

🇮🇩 Pola: (puluhan)じゅう + (satuan). Jauh lebih teratur daripada Indonesia ("sebelas, dua puluh").

### 2. Big numbers — 百 / 千 / 万

| Japanese | Romaji | Value |
|----------|--------|-------|
| ひゃく | *hyaku* | 100 |
| せん | *sen* | 1,000 |
| まん | *man* | 10,000 |

```
300  = さんびゃく   (sanbyaku — sound change!)
1,000 = せん
8,000 = はっせん
50,000 = ごまん
```

⚠️ Watch the **sound changes**: 300 = さん**び**ゃく, 600 = ろっぴゃく, 800 = はっぴゃく. Don't worry about memorizing all now — recognize them when you hear them.

⚠️ **Japanese counts in units of 10,000 (万), not 1,000.** So 100,000 = じゅうまん ("10 man"), not "hundred-thousand". This trips up every learner — go slow with big prices.

### 3. Prices: number + 円

```
これは     ひゃくえん   です。
kore wa    hyaku-en     desu
🇮🇩 "Ini 100 yen."
```
- **えん** (*en*) = 円 = yen. Always comes after the number.

### 4. Asking the price: いくら

```
これは いくらですか。
kore wa ikura desu ka
🇮🇩 "Ini berapa harganya?"
```

### 5. Buying it: 〜を ください

```
これを      ください。
kore o      kudasai
🇮🇩 "Tolong (beri) ini." → "Saya mau ini."
```
- **を** (*o*) = object particle (written を, pronounced *o*). Marks the thing you want.
- **ください** (*kudasai*) = "please give me". Super useful and polite.

## 💬 Dialogue — at the konbini (コンビニ で)

**みせのひと (clerk):** いらっしゃいませ！
🇮🇩 Selamat datang! *(set phrase, you don't reply to it)*

**あなた:** すみません、これは いくらですか。
🇮🇩 Permisi, ini berapa?

**みせのひと:** にひゃくごじゅうえん です。
*Nihyaku-gojū-en desu.*
🇮🇩 250 yen.

**あなた:** じゃあ、これを ください。
🇮🇩 Kalau begitu, saya mau ini.

**みせのひと:** ありがとうございます。
🇮🇩 Terima kasih.

💡 **いらっしゃいませ** = "welcome", said to every customer. You just nod — no reply needed.
💡 **じゃあ** (*jā*) = "kalau begitu / well then".

---

## 🏯 Culture note — money in Japan

- **Japan is still very cash-based**, though IC cards (Suica/PASMO) and QR pay are growing. Know your coins: 1, 5, 10, 50, 100, 500 円 coins; 1,000 / 5,000 / 10,000 円 notes.
- **There's usually a little tray** at the register — put your money there rather than into the clerk's hand.
- **No tipping.** Ever. It can even cause confusion.
- **Tax (税込/税抜):** prices may be 税込 (*zeikomi*, tax included) or 税抜 (*zeinuki*, before tax). The big number is often pre-tax — check the small print.
