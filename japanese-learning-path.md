# 🇯🇵 Japanese Learning Path (for Indonesian speakers)

> **Your starting point:** Native Indonesian · can read hiragana · a little katakana
> **Your goal:** 🏯 Living / studying in Japan — balanced all-round (speaking + reading + formal grammar)
> **Your pace:** ~45–60 min/day (the standard routine below fits this exactly)
> **Path:** kana → grammar → kanji → conversation → real-life Japan survival.

---

## 🎌 Good news for you (Indonesian advantages)

- **Vowels are almost identical.** Japanese `あいうえお` = `a i u e o`, same as Indonesian. You already have the right mouth.
- **No tones.** Unlike Chinese, pitch matters only a little. Indonesian habits transfer well.
- **Romaji reads like Indonesian.** `sushi`, `arigatou`, `sakura` — you pronounce these naturally already.
- **Sentence rhythm is syllable-based**, like Indonesian. Easier than English speakers have it.

**Watch out for:** word order is **Subject–Object–Verb** (verb goes last), and Japanese uses *particles* (は を に で) instead of word order to mark grammar — this is the biggest new concept.

---

## 📍 Stage 0 — Finish your kana (1–2 weeks)

You're close. Lock these down before grammar:

- [ ] All **katakana** (カタカナ) — same speed as hiragana
- [ ] **Dakuten / handakuten**: が ざ だ ば ぱ (゛ ゜)
- [ ] **Combos (youon)**: きゃ しゅ ちょ etc.
- [ ] **Small tsu (っ)** = double consonant: がっこう = *gakkou*
- [ ] **Long vowels**: おう / うう / ー

**Tools:** [Kana app "Kana (Hiragana/Katakana)"], [Tofugu kana guide], or Anki kana deck.
**Drill:** read katakana menus/brand names — コーヒー (coffee), テレビ (TV), アイスクリーム (ice cream).

---

## 📍 Stage 1 — Core grammar foundation (2–3 months)

This is where real Japanese starts. Don't rush — this stage decides everything.

**Main textbook (pick ONE):**
- **Genki I** — most popular, structured, great for beginners ⭐ recommended
- **Tae Kim's Guide to Japanese** — free online, also has community Indonesian translations

**Concepts to master in order:**
1. です / だ (to be), basic sentences: これは本です ("this is a book")
2. Particles: は (topic), が (subject), を (object), に / へ (direction), で (place/means), と (and/with)
3. Verb groups & conjugation: ます-form, dictionary form, て-form, past, negative
4. Adjectives: い-adjectives vs な-adjectives
5. Question words: なに、だれ、どこ、いつ、なぜ、どう
6. Counting & numbers (very different system — practice early)

**Habit:** 1 grammar point/day + 10 min review. Use [Bunpro] to track grammar SRS.

---

## 📍 Stage 2 — Kanji + Vocabulary (start in parallel with Stage 1)

Don't wait — begin kanji as soon as you start grammar.

- **Start with JLPT N5 kanji** (~100 characters): 日 月 火 水 木 金 土 人 大 小 ...
- **Tool:** [WaniKani] (kanji + vocab via radicals) OR an [Anki] "Core 2000" deck (most common words)
- **Target:** 5–10 new items/day, review daily. Consistency > volume.

**Vocabulary goal by end of Stage 2:** ~800–1000 words + ~100 kanji = enough for simple daily life.

---

## 📍 Stage 3 — Input & immersion (ongoing from month 2)

Listening and reading make grammar *stick*.

- **Reading:** [NHK News Web Easy] (easy news with furigana), [Tadoku graded readers] (free, leveled)
- **Listening:** [Nihongo con Teppei] podcast (beginner), Japanese YouTube with JP subtitles
- **Watch:** anime/drama with **Japanese** subtitles (not just Indonesian/English)
- **Rule:** comprehensible input — material where you understand ~80%.

---

## 📍 Stage 4 — Output & JLPT progression

- **Speaking/writing:** language exchange apps — [HelloTalk], [Tandem]; write a daily 3-sentence diary in Japanese
- **JLPT roadmap** (official proficiency test):
  - **N5** — basic (target: ~6–12 months in)
  - **N4** — elementary
  - **N3** — intermediate (conversational threshold)
  - **N2 / N1** — advanced / fluent

---

## 🏯 Extra focus for LIVING in Japan

Because your goal is to actually live/study there, weave these in from Stage 2 onward — they matter more for you than for a casual learner:

- [ ] **Polite forms (keigo) basics** — Japan runs on politeness levels. Master です/ます early, then ていねい語 → 尊敬語/謙譲語 later. You'll need this for landlords, offices, school, shops.
- [ ] **Survival kanji on sight** — signs you'll see daily: 出口 (exit) 入口 (entrance) 止まれ (stop) 危険 (danger) 駅 (station) 男/女 (men/women) 営業中 (open) 押す/引く (push/pull) 無料 (free) 円 (yen).
- [ ] **Situational scripts** — practice full dialogues for: convenience store/cashier, train ticket & IC card, restaurant ordering, doctor/pharmacy, ward office (市役所) paperwork, opening a bank account, asking directions.
- [ ] **Numbers & counters fluently** — money (円), time, dates, addresses, phone numbers. Critical for daily life.
- [ ] **Reading practice on real things** — menus, station maps, apartment listings, recycling/trash rules (ゴミの分別 is real homework in Japan!).

> Tip: If studying at a Japanese language school, target **JLPT N4–N3** before arrival, and N2 for university/work.

## 🗓️ Suggested daily routine (~45–60 min)

| Time | Activity |
|------|----------|
| 10 min | Kanji + vocab SRS review (Anki/WaniKani) |
| 15 min | New grammar point (Genki/Tae Kim) + exercises |
| 15 min | Listening or reading (NHK Easy, podcast) |
| 10 min | Output: write 2–3 sentences or shadow audio |

**Golden rule:** 30 minutes every day beats 4 hours once a week. SRS punishes gaps.

---

## ✅ 90-day milestones

- **Day 30:** All kana fluent · ~150 words · particles は/を/が · basic です sentences
- **Day 60:** て-form & past tense · ~400 words · ~50 kanji · read NHK Easy headlines
- **Day 90:** Self-introduction & daily small talk · ~800 words · ~100 kanji · ready to start N5 prep
